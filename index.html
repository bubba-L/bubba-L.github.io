Projects

Minerva's Marvelous Mixturette

January - May 2024

Minerva's Marvelous Mixturette is a keyboard-controlled cooking game. You play as Minerva, a witch running a small potion business out of a carriage. The goal is to serve customers their orders as quickly and accurately as possible.

This game was made in MonoGame by Witch Works - a group of four students including myself - for the course Game Development and Algorithm Problem Solving II, and was worked on throughout the semester.

My main focuses included working on the file I/O, loading in level information and saving/showing the highest scores for each level. I also worked specifically on the UI of the score and level select screens, as well as writing, balancing, and playtesting the levels. The whole group contributed to planning, documentation, code oragnization, and basic functions of gameplay.

Rose from the Dead

January - May 2024

Rose from the Dead is a 2D platformer game whose story features Carrik - the playable character - whose village depends on flowers that give holder different abilities. These flowers are dying and her village is defenseless without them, so Carrik is determined to find out what is causing them to die.

This project was made throughout a semester for the course 2D Animation and Asset Production. The game was made using Unity's 2D platformer template, with all of the art, assets, and animations made by me, along with a title cutscene animation. For this project I used a combination of Procreate, Adobe PhotoShop and AfterEffects, Krita, Unity, and Davinchi Resolve.

Fruit Ninja Remake

September - October 2024

This game is a replication of the popular game “Fruit Ninja”, with different food items as the fruit and an angry-looking fish as the bombs. The player has three lives and gains points by cutting the food and avoiding the fish. When the player holds the left mouse button and swipes across the food or fish being launched across the screen, that game object is cut and disappears, affecting the player’s points and lives according to what object was cut (or missed).

Developed a recreation of the game Fruit Ninja in Unity, using Unity’s built in physics, input system, and components, as well as C# scripts that control object spawning, launching, destroying, mouse tracking, and point system.

The goal of the game is to try and cut the food items as they fly across the screen but avoid hitting the fish. The items can be cut by holding down the left mouse button and swiping over the game object. Cutting a food item gains the player one point, and missing one takes away three points. The player has three lives, and they lose a life if they cut a fish or if their points hit zero. Once the player runs out of lives, a game over screen pops up and the food stops launching. The player can then press the left mouse button to restart the game.

The food and fish are launched across the screen from a randomly chosen spawn point (out of four possible points), towards a random target (of three choices), at a random frequency based on how long the game has ran, and with a random sprite and force. The sprite determines the effects that the game object has on the player's points and lives, and the range of possible forces depends on the distance the object has to travel. The randomization increases the difficulty of the game and allows for the game to become more difficult over time.

Collision detection is used to track points, lives, and when to destroy game objects, and the player can only cut objects when swiping with the left mouse button pressed, as the collision component is disabled otherwise. The cursor that follows the mouse, with a sword sprite, collides with the game objects and triggers the points/lives to change, as well as destroying the object. A platform beneath the spawning platform collides with objects that were not cut and have fallen, and destroys them as well as deducting the points for a missed food item.
